Section 03 · All Checks · Universal Library

The "why this matters" behind every check.

Universal best-practice entries for the 17 checks Web Cited runs. Each entry explains what the check measures, why it moves LLM citation, and how to fix it. Reusable reference for your team regardless of how Claimzilla scored on this audit.

Citation · LLM

OpenAI / Anthropic / Gemini / Perplexity citation rate

For each buyer question, send the same prompt to all four engines and capture which engines name your domain in the response. Aggregate across the prompt grid for a per-engine score.

Why it matters: the four engines collectively cover ~95% of generative-answer market share. A brand that's cited by 3 of 4 engines on the buyer's question is the de facto answer.

Fix levers: authoritative content matching the buyer's exact phrasing, third-party citations on Wikipedia + niche publications, FAQ schema, llms.txt with priority pages.
Discoverability

llms.txt at site root

Public spec for telling LLM crawlers what to prioritize and what to skip. One URL per line, optional # notes. Most LLM crawlers (Anthropic, Perplexity, OpenAI's GPTBot) honor it within 14 days of publication.

Fix: publish 8 to 12 priority URLs at /llms.txt. Include homepage, /how-it-works, /faq, top buyer-decision pages, and authoritative reference content.
SERP · AIO

Google AI Overview placement

For commercial buyer queries, Google now shows an AI-generated multi-source summary above organic results. The brands cited in the AIO get the highest-conversion answer slot in Google search. Coverage is the % of your buyer queries where your domain appears.

Fix: earn FAQPage rich result eligibility (AIO draws from FAQ-marked content disproportionately), publish answer-first paragraphs (90 to 110 words) on the queries you want to capture, earn 2 to 3 mid-authority third-party citations per query topic.
Structured data

JSON-LD: Organization, WebSite, Service, FAQPage

Schema.org JSON-LD is the most reliable way to feed your entity facts to LLM training pipelines + Google's Knowledge Graph. Four priority shapes: Organization (who you are), WebSite (your site as a publisher), Service or Product (what you sell), FAQPage (your buyer's most-searched questions).

Fix: ship all four shapes on your homepage / category / FAQ pages. Validate via Google's Rich Results Test before merging. Per-page samples in your Schema Pack tab.
Entity · Knowledge Graph

Google Knowledge Graph entity entry

Google maintains a graph of entities (companies, people, places). Brands with a complete Knowledge Graph entry win the right-rail Knowledge Panel on branded searches and feed Gemini's grounding signal heavily.

Fix: submit a Schema.org Organization update with sameAs (LinkedIn, Crunchbase, Wikipedia, Wikidata), founder, foundingDate, address, contactPoint. Allow 14 to 30 days for KG to absorb.
Entity · Wikidata

Wikidata Q-ID + typed claims

Wikidata is the multilingual structured-fact graph that feeds both Wikipedia's right-rail panels and most LLM entity-grounding pipelines. A Q-ID with typed claims (instance-of, industry, founded, etc.) is the single strongest entity-graph signal for Anthropic Claude.

Fix: open or expand a Wikidata item (community-edited; takes 2 to 3 weeks to settle). Populate at least 8 to 10 typed claims. Link sameAs identifiers (LinkedIn, Crunchbase, X handle).
Entity · Wikipedia

Wikipedia article depth + external citations

LLMs weight Wikipedia article depth heavily when deciding whether to name a brand by name vs by category. Articles below 600 words with under 5 third-party citations rarely survive LLM citation generation.

Fix: editorial push to expand the article to 600+ words covering history, model, funding, market position. Cite 5+ reliable third-party sources (industry publications, regulator filings, Forbes / TechCrunch / WSJ coverage). 2-week effort; lasting payoff.
SEO · Fundamentals

Title, description, canonical, robots, viewport, H1, H2, lang

The eight on-page fundamentals every page must get right. Title under 60 chars, description 130 to 160 chars, canonical pointing to the preferred URL, robots index/follow, viewport mobile-friendly, single H1 per page, hierarchical H2s, lang attribute set on the html element.

Fix: a CMS template lock-down ensures every published page hits all 8. Audit existing top-50 traffic pages for compliance; the typical hit rate before lock-down is 60-70%.
Meta & social

Open Graph + Twitter Card completeness

Nine OG tags + four Twitter Card tags drive how your link previews render across Facebook, LinkedIn, Slack, iMessage, X, Discord. Incomplete preview cards lose ~20% of share click-through versus fully-populated ones.

Fix: validate every page-template via OpenGraph.xyz and LinkedIn Post Inspector before going live. Preview image must be 1200x630 px and under 1 MB.
Media

YouTube embed transcripts

Embedded videos without <track> caption files OR visible transcript text below the video are invisible to LLM crawlers (which can't transcribe video content). Critical for video-heavy landing pages.

Fix: auto-generate captions in YouTube Studio, review for accuracy on industry terms, publish as a public caption track. Bonus: embed the transcript as visible page text in a collapsible <details> element.
Media

YouTube mentions in third-party content

Brand mentioned in titles, descriptions, or transcripts of YouTube videos the brand didn't publish. Among the single strongest signals for being named when an LLM is asked an open-ended buyer question.

Fix: pitch product reviews / explainer videos to mid-tier YouTube personal-finance / industry channels (50K to 500K subscribers). Each video lifts both general-search citation and Perplexity grounding.
SERP · Bing

Bing Copilot answer + organic rank

Bing has ~10% of US search and feeds Microsoft Copilot, which is the second-largest source of LLM grounding traffic after Google. Bing weights structured data + freshness more than Google.

Fix: keep page lastmod headers + sitemap.xml lastmod fresh on every content update. Use the IndexNow API to push updates to Bing within minutes instead of waiting for the crawl cycle. Most comparison sites neglect IndexNow.

Glossary

  • SXO Search Experience Optimization. The umbrella discipline covering SEO + AEO + GEO; named so because end-state is buyer experience across every answer surface, not a single channel. Web Cited
  • SEO Search Engine Optimization. Classical practice of ranking organic results in Google + Bing search engine results pages. Tab 03
  • AEO Answer Engine Optimization. Optimizing for direct-answer surfaces like Google's AI Overview, Bing Copilot, and zero-click search results. Tab 03
  • GEO Generative Engine Optimization. Optimizing for being cited by name when an LLM (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Perplexity) generates an answer to a buyer's question. Tab 03
  • AIO Google AI Overview: the multi-source summary that appears above organic results in Google Search. Highest-conversion answer slot in Google today. Tab 04
  • FAQPage schema Schema.org type marking Q&A content as structured data. Eligible for FAQ rich-result + AIO inclusion. One of the highest-leverage schemas to ship. Tab 05
  • JSON-LD JavaScript Object Notation for Linked Data. The structured-data syntax Google + Bing prefer; lives in a <script type="application/ld+json"> tag in the page <head>. Tab 05
  • Schema.org Multi-vendor consortium-maintained vocabulary for structured data on the web. Defines types like Organization, WebSite, Service, Article, FAQPage, etc. Tab 05
  • llms.txt Public spec for telling LLM crawlers which URLs to prioritize. One URL per line at the site root. Honored by Anthropic, Perplexity, OpenAI's GPTBot. Tab 04
  • Knowledge Graph Google's structured database of entities (companies, people, places). Appears as the right-rail Knowledge Panel for branded queries; feeds Gemini's grounding. Tab 03
  • Wikidata Q-ID Stable identifier (e.g., Q12399847) tying a brand to typed claims in Wikidata. Strongest entity-graph signal for Anthropic Claude. Tab 03
  • Citation share Percent of buyer-question prompts where at least one of the four LLM engines names your domain in the answer. Tab 01
  • Leader share The same metric for the highest-cited competitor in your benchmark set. Usually one of the 2 to 4 competitors named at intake. Tab 01
  • BreadcrumbList Schema.org type that marks page hierarchy (Home » Category » Page) so Google can show breadcrumbs in search results and feed them to LLMs. Tab 05
  • Open Graph Facebook-originated meta-tag spec controlling how a URL renders when shared on social platforms. Now adopted across LinkedIn, Slack, iMessage, X, Discord. Tab 03
  • Twitter Card X-specific share-preview meta tags. Augments Open Graph; summary_large_image is the default modern type. Tab 03
  • CWV (Core Web Vitals) Google's three perceived-performance metrics: LCP (Largest Contentful Paint), INP (Interaction to Next Paint), CLS (Cumulative Layout Shift). Affects both ranking and perceived quality. Tab 03
  • SERP Search Engine Results Page. The page Google or Bing returns for a query; mix of organic, AIO, ads, knowledge panels, image carousels. Tab 03
  • Severity ladder High (failing signal that directly blocks citation), Medium (partial signal that reduces probability), Clean (signal present and well-formed), Info (observation, not scored). Tab 02
  • Buyer question A real question your prospects ask LLMs (e.g., "What is the cheapest car insurance for X?"). Audit measures how often each LLM names your domain across the buyer-question grid. Tab 01

FAQ

How long does my Playbook stay accessible?

12 months from delivery date. The URL is unguessable (16 random digits with hyphens; CSPRNG-minted) and the secret is the URL itself; no auth, no email gate. Share it freely with your SEO and engineering leads. After 12 months it returns 410 Gone.

Can I share the Playbook URL?

Yes. Treat it like any internal-only doc URL. Per the slug-entropy math (16 digits = 53 bits), brute-forcing a valid slug is computationally infeasible at any realistic attack scale, so the URL is safe to share inside your team without auth in front of it.

How do I re-run the audit after fixing things?

What's the difference between Pulse, SXO Audit, and Enterprise tiers?

Pulse ($1500): per-page schema pack + PDF, 10 URLs audited, 3-business-day delivery. SXO Audit ($5000): adds the full Playbook, 25 URLs, 5-business-day delivery. Enterprise ($12,500): 1 to 3 brands at SXO Audit depth + a Portfolio Playbook tying the brand findings together, 10-business-day delivery.

Why JSON-LD instead of microdata or RDFa?

Google prefers JSON-LD; Bing accepts all three but treats JSON-LD as canonical when multiple are present. JSON-LD lives in a single <script> tag and doesn't require restructuring the page DOM, so it's the lowest-risk path to ship.

How do I know if my fixes actually moved the needle?

When 3 of 4 engines cite you on a buyer-question prompt where they previously didn't, that's a confirmed lift.
