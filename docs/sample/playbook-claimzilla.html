The three patterns moving the needle

High
Google AI Overview slot is held by competitors

0 of 8 commercial buyer queries surfaced Claimzilla in the AIO. Insurify holds 5 of 8; The Zebra holds 2; NerdWallet holds 1.

Fix
Earn FAQPage rich result + answer-first paragraphs (90 to 110 words) on the 8 missed queries. AIO draws from FAQ-marked content disproportionately.
High
/llms.txt is missing

Crawlers fall back to robots.txt heuristics when /llms.txt is absent. That sampling tends to grab low-value comparison-form pages instead of the homepage + how-it-works + research hub.

Fix
Publish /llms.txt at the site root with 8 to 12 priority URLs. Most LLM crawlers honor it within 14 days.
Medium
Wikipedia article depth is below LLM-grounding threshold

240 words + 2 third-party citations. Competitors range 8 to 21 cites. Anthropic + OpenAI weight Wikipedia depth heavily when deciding whether to name a brand vs name a competitor.

Section 07 · Resources

Glossary & reference links.

Six terms that recur in the audit, with one-line definitions and the canonical reference link.

  • AIO Google AI Overview: the multi-source summary that appears at the top of Google search results. Section 04
  • FAQPage schema Schema.org type that marks Q&A content as structured data, eligible for FAQ-rich-result and AIO inclusion. Section 05
  • llms.txt Public spec for telling LLM crawlers which URLs to prioritize. One URL per line at the site root. Section 04
  • Knowledge Graph Google's entity database; appears as the right-rail Knowledge Panel for branded queries. Section 03
  • Wikidata Q-ID Stable identifier (e.g., Q12399847) tying a brand to typed claims feeding LLM entity graphs. Section 03
  • Citation share Percent of buyer-question prompts where at least one of the four LLM engines names your domain in the answer. Section 01
