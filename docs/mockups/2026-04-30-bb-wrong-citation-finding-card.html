Item BB: WRONG_CITATION finding-card category (Playbook + PDF)

Context. Item BB introduces a new severity WRONG_CITATION for findings where an LLM cited the brand BUT WITH FALSE FACTS (e.g., "Cepheid manufactures dental imaging equipment"). Today these are silently classified as positive citations by the substring matcher. The new severity sits between FAIL (the brand was missed entirely) and WARN (the brand was cited weakly) and needs its own color token + chip + finding-card border-left styling. Spec at web-cited-pipeline/docs/superpowers/specs/2026-04-29-item-bb-brainstorm-prep.md.

All variants reuse canonical finding-card structure and chip typography (verbatim from playbook-claimzilla.html). Only the --wrong color token + .is-wrong rule are new.

Variant A: new --wrong magenta/purple color (RECOMMENDED)

Proposed Variant A

New token --wrong: #7d2a7d (magenta/purple). Visually distinct from --fail accent-red while still alarming. Good contrast on white finding-card background. The wrong-citation card sits alongside existing FAIL / WARN / PASS cards with its own 6px border-left and chip color.

Recommendation

Variant A. WRONG_CITATION is genuinely a different problem class from FAIL (the brand was cited, but with false facts). A distinct color token tells the customer at a glance that this row needs different remediation than the missing-citation FAIL rows. The magenta/purple #7d2a7d stays in the existing brand color family (alarming, but not the same accent-red as the FAIL chip).

Variant D (scorecard panel) is a strong second-best for the operator who wants the smallest visual surface change; recommend pairing Variant A finding cards with Variant D's per-engine panel as a Section 02 Scorecard companion.
