## Edit 3 (NEW): §3.3 Citation Monitor subscription

### Proposed new section (insert after §3.2, before §4)

> **§3.3 Citation Monitor subscription.**
>
> Web Cited offers a separately scoped, recurring Citation Monitor subscription at $99 per calendar month, charged monthly in advance via Stripe. The Citation Monitor is a lighter-scope product than the 12-Month Monitoring add-on described in Section 3.2 and is not an audit. The Citation Monitor is purchasable without a prior audit purchase; purchase of a Citation Monitor subscription does not include any audit deliverable.
>
> **Subscription scope.** Each monthly Citation Monitor reading runs one (1) buyer-research prompt of your choice across four large language model engines (currently OpenAI ChatGPT, Anthropic Claude, Google Gemini, and Perplexity Sonar), with three (3) trials per engine for variance, against your brand domain and up to three (3) competitor domains you supply. Each reading produces (a) a citation share percentage per engine with a 95% Wilson confidence interval, (b) a competitor citation breakdown, (c) a delta versus the previous month's reading, and (d) a rolling 12-month trend chart accessible from your subscription dashboard. The Citation Monitor does not include the Audit Report PDF, the Playbook, the Schema Pack, the additional buyer prompts of the audit tiers, or the 30-day async Q&A window of the audit tiers.
>
> **Cadence.** One automatic reading is run on each monthly billing anniversary. You may additionally trigger up to three (3) on-demand re-runs per billing cycle from your dashboard. On-demand re-runs that exceed the three-per-cycle limit are rejected with a message indicating when the next on-demand re-run will become available; they do not roll over.
>
> **Auto-renewal and cancellation.** The subscription auto-renews each month on your billing anniversary date until cancelled. You may cancel at any time from the Stripe-hosted billing portal accessible from each monthly email or by emailing hello@web-cited.com with the subject "Cancel Citation Monitor". Cancellation takes effect at the end of the current billing month: future readings and future charges stop, but the in-progress month's reading remains available to you, and no prorated refund is issued for the current month. Cancellations are processed within one (1) Business Day of receipt; if your billing date passes before the cancellation is processed, the next month's charge will be refunded as a goodwill exception. See Section 4.8 for the full cancellation mechanics.
>
> **Promotional first-month pricing.** Web Cited may, in its discretion, offer promotional first-month pricing (for example, "first month at $1" or "first month free") on the Citation Monitor subscription. Promotional pricing applies only to the first calendar month after subscription start; subsequent months bill at the then-current standard subscription rate of $99 per calendar month. Promotional pricing is non-transferable and one per customer; Web Cited may decline a promotional rate to any customer in its discretion. Cancellation during a promotional first month stops future billing without further charge.
>
> **No refunds.** The Citation Monitor subscription is a recurring service delivered monthly. Once a monthly reading has been generated and made available in your dashboard, that month's $99 charge is non-refundable except as required by applicable law. The Section 4.5 work-commencement-trigger and the Section 4.6 turnaround remedies do not apply to the Citation Monitor subscription. The Section 4.7 deliverable-acceptance clause does not apply to the Citation Monitor; each monthly reading is deemed accepted upon delivery.
>
> **Substantive distinction from audit tiers.** Citation Monitor is not a substitute for the SXO Audit or Enterprise audit. It does not include the comprehensive audit methodology, the Playbook, the Schema Pack, the technical SEO checks, the schema validation, the entity verification, or the off-site content audit. Customers seeking those deliverables must purchase the corresponding audit tier under Section 3.1.
>
> **Substantive distinction from 12-Month Monitoring add-on.** Citation Monitor is also not a substitute for the 12-Month Monitoring add-on described in Section 3.2. The §3.2 add-on uses the full 5-buyer-prompt audit methodology, includes Playbook updates each month, and is sold as a one-time upfront payment covering twelve (12) months. The §3.3 Citation Monitor uses a single buyer prompt, does not include Playbook updates, and is billed monthly at a substantially lower rate.

### Rationale

A new entry-tier recurring product. Substantively differentiated from both the audit tiers (which it does not include) and the 12-Month Monitoring add-on (which is heavier and bundled with the audit). Cancel-anytime mechanics align with California consumer-protection norms for monthly subscriptions; no proration is industry-standard for monthly SaaS.

## Edit 4 (NEW): §4.8 Subscription cancellation

### Proposed new section (insert after §4.7, before §5)

> **§4.8 Subscription cancellation.**
>
> This Section 4.8 applies to the Citation Monitor subscription under Section 3.3. It does not apply to one-time audit engagements (Section 3.1) or to the 12-Month Monitoring add-on (Section 3.2), each of which is governed by its respective section.
>
> **How to cancel.** You may cancel an active Citation Monitor subscription at any time by either (a) using the "Cancel subscription" link in any monthly email or in the Stripe-hosted billing portal accessible at https://billing.stripe.com (or such successor URL as Web Cited may publish), or (b) emailing hello@web-cited.com with the subject "Cancel Citation Monitor" from the email address associated with the subscription.
>
> **When cancellation takes effect.** Cancellation takes effect at the end of the current billing month, defined as the calendar day immediately preceding your next monthly billing anniversary. The in-progress month's reading remains available to you until the end of the billing month, and Section 9 confidentiality protections continue to apply to the readings indefinitely.
>
> **No proration; no refund for partial months.** Web Cited does not prorate the $99 monthly charge for cancellations made mid-month. You will not be refunded any portion of the current month's $99 charge upon cancellation, regardless of when in the billing cycle the cancellation is processed. This is consistent with industry-standard practice for monthly software subscriptions and is the consideration for the cancel-anytime flexibility provided in Section 3.3.
>
> **Goodwill exception for processing-delay charges.** If Web Cited receives a cancellation request and the next monthly billing anniversary passes before the cancellation is processed (resulting in a charge for a month after cancellation was requested), Web Cited will refund that subsequent month's charge as a goodwill exception, provided the cancellation request was received in good faith and before the disputed billing date.
>
> **Failed payment retries.** If a monthly Citation Monitor charge fails (for example, expired card, insufficient funds, or 3D Secure challenge unmet), Stripe's standard Smart Retries logic applies: typically up to four (4) retry attempts over fourteen (14) days. If all retries fail, the subscription is automatically suspended and you will be notified by email. The subscription resumes upon successful payment of the missed month. If the suspended period exceeds thirty (30) days, the subscription is cancelled automatically, with no obligation on either party to reinstate.
>
> **Effect of cancellation on stored data.** Cancellation does not, by itself, delete your stored readings, your subscription metadata, or your CRM record. Deletion of stored data is governed by Section 5.6 (Customer Content deletion) and may be requested at any time, including alongside or after a subscription cancellation, via the mechanism set out in Section 5.6.

### Rationale

Cancel-anytime, no proration, with Stripe Smart Retries for failed payments is the prevailing monthly-SaaS standard. The goodwill-exception clause prevents disputes over processing-delay charges. The "stored data is governed by §5.6" clause clarifies that cancellation is a billing action, not a data-deletion request, while preserving customer agency to request both.

## Summary for the attorney

These edits do two things: retire one product (Pulse + Pulse Monitor) and add one recurring subscription (Citation Monitor at $99/mo with auto-renewal and cancel-anytime mechanics). The existing audit tiers and the existing 12-Month Monitoring add-on are unchanged. California consumer-protection considerations for the recurring subscription are addressed via the cancel-anytime, no-proration, and goodwill-exception clauses. Failed-payment handling uses Stripe Smart Retries. Stored-data deletion remains governed by §5.6.

**Effective date.** The revised Terms become effective upon publication on https://web-cited.com/terms.html (current convention). The "Last updated" date at the top of terms.html is bumped to the publication date. No existing customer is grandfathered out of the new sections because (a) the Pulse retirement does not affect any prior engagement (every prior engagement was governed by the version of the TOS in effect at the time of payment, which remains the contract for that engagement) and (b) the Citation Monitor subscription is a new product with no prior customers as of the proposed effective date.

**Questions for the attorney's review.**

1. Is the cancel-anytime + no-proration language adequate under California's Automatic Renewal Law (Business and Professions Code §17600 et seq.)?
2. Is the failed-payment Smart Retries clause adequate disclosure of subscription suspension and automatic termination?
3. Is the "promotional first-month pricing" language sufficient or does California consumer-protection law require more explicit "your card will be charged $99 on [date] unless you cancel before then" wording at the point of subscription?
4. The cancel-anytime clause says "Cancellation takes effect at the end of the current billing month." Should this instead read "at the end of the current billing period" for clarity, given that "billing month" could be ambiguous if a customer changes plans mid-subscription?
5. Should the §4.8 stored-data clause cross-reference §5.6 more strongly (e.g., "for the avoidance of doubt, cancellation does NOT delete stored data; you must separately request deletion")?
